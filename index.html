    How to use Recall

    1. Load a Deck

    Open the menu (☰, top-left) to get started:

    • + New Deck — start a blank deck and add cards manually
    • Import from File — paste Markdown, upload a .md/.json/.zip file, or fetch from a URL
    • My Decks — every deck you've studied, saved on this device and mirrored to the cloud

    Study Notes 📝 — learn first, card later

    Every deck has a freeform Notes document beside its cards. Use the Cards ⇄ Notes toggle at the top of the study area (or Study Notes in the toolbar).

    • Write notes first — full Markdown: headings, math, diagrams, code, pasted images
    • Highlight → + Make card — select any fact (text, images, math) in the rendered notes; the floating button shows how much is captured. Tap it to preview the selection as the card's answer and frame the question that should bring it to mind (Ctrl+Enter to add)
    • Notes save, sync, and export together with the deck — or skip notes entirely and make cards directly, as before

    2. Study Cards

    5. Edit & Format Cards

    Click the pencil icon on the question or answer face — or long-press the card with a mouse — to edit. Click the save icon to save. Edits are always committed when you navigate away. On phones and tablets, long-press selects text — use the pencil icon to edit.

    While editing, a formatting toolbar appears above the text. Select some text, then click a button to wrap it:

    B I U SBold, Italic, Underline, Strikethrough
    </>Inline code
    […]Cloze — hide the selection as a fill-in-the-blank. In the editor it wraps the selection in {{ }}; in the rendered card or notes, select some text and tap the […] button to cloze it in place (tap again on the same text to un-cloze). Tap a cloze on the card to reveal it, or use the 👀 Reveal clozes button to flip them all at once (press again to hide them all; you can still tap any single one)
    B I UFormat-in-place toolbar — the row above each rendered card face and the notes preview formats the selected text without entering edit mode: bold, italic, underline, strikethrough, code, cloze, plus a text-colour and a highlight split button. Click the paint/marker button to apply the current default colour in one tap; click its ▾ side (which shows the current colour) to pick a different one — your pick becomes the new default

    Images 🖼️

    Add images three ways while editing a card:

    • Paste — copy any image (a screenshot, or an image from a webpage) and press Ctrl+V in the editor
    • Drag & drop — drag an image file onto the editor (desktop)
    • Toolbar 🖼️ — tap the image button to open your file picker. On phones and tablets this offers your camera or photo library

    Each image is optimized in your browser (downscaled to 1600px max and re-encoded to WebP) before being uploaded to your own Supabase Storage bucket; an ![](…) link is then inserted automatically. No separate key to set up — the same sign-in that unlocks sync unlocks image uploads. Images need a connection to upload. Hover a rendered image and use its 🗑 button to remove it from the note — since it's your own storage, that also deletes the file for good, unlike the old ImgBB host.

    Google Drive links just work: paste a public share link like ![](https://drive.google.com/file/d/FILE_ID/view) and it renders directly — no need to convert it first.

    6. Quick Notes 📌

    Found something worth keeping while reviewing an answer? Capture it without leaving your deck:

    • Open the answer in edit mode (pencil icon — long-press works on desktop — then flip to the answer side)
    • Select the text you want to keep
    • Click the 📌 button at the end of the formatting toolbar

    The selection is saved as a new card in a dedicated quick_notes cloud deck (created automatically the first time). The selected text becomes the card’s question, with a blank answer for you to fill in later. A ✓ Saved to quick_notes toast confirms it worked. Open your quick_notes deck any time from My Decks. (Requires sign-in, since notes are stored in the cloud.)

    7. Track Progress

    The coloured bar at the top of the card area shows your session progress:

    Known Review Progress

    The header shows a live Known X / Review Y count, and each card carries a status badge. Replay filtered sessions (Review only, Known only, Uncategorized) from the buttons below the card.

    8. All Cards Panel

    Click to see every card in a scrollable Cornell-note view. You can edit, delete, or reorder cards from here.

    9. Sync & Cloud

    • Sync is automatic — every deck is mirrored to your account in the background; Sync Now forces an immediate two-way reconcile (newest copy of each deck wins)
    • My Decks — every deck, on this device and in the cloud, with a live sync-status column. Per deck: Load, Export (PDF / Markdown / JSON / SQL), Rename, Delete, and an inline category editor. Filter by category, select several decks for bulk Load / Categorize / Export / Delete, or Export All to back up the whole library. Deletes propagate to your other devices
    • Export Cards… (menu) — the current deck as Cornell PDF, Markdown, JSON, or SQL

    Every cloud action gives you instant feedback: a toast slides in at the top of the screen confirming it worked — for example ✓ Deck deleted everywhere or ✕ Sync failed — so you never have to wonder whether your sync, load, or delete went through. Click a toast to dismiss it early.

    10. Offline & My Decks 💾

    Once you’ve opened the app online once, it keeps working with no connection:

    • You stay signed in offline (after signing in online at least once), so you go straight to your decks.
    • Saving is automatic — every edit is written to the on-device library in the background; there is no manual save button. A reload starts on the clean home screen; reopen your deck from My Decks.
    • Every cloud deck is mirrored onto the device automatically, so the whole library is available offline — load, rename, or delete with no connection.
    • An Offline badge appears bottom-left when you lose connection.

    A first-time login needs internet. Edits made offline (including deletes) sync automatically once the connection returns.

    11. Card Format

    When importing or pasting Markdown, use the :: separator format:

    ::
What is the capital of France?
---
Paris
::

::
What year was the Eiffel Tower built?
---
1889
::

    You can also use ##, ###, or #### headings as card fronts, with the content below as the back.

    12. Themes & Style

    Click to choose from 10 built-in themes, adjust font size, card radius, and more. Desktop and mobile save separate style profiles.
